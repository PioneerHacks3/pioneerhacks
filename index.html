FAQ

What is PioneerHacks?

At a hackathon, attendees learn, build, & share their creations over the course of a weekend. Despite being entirely online this year, PioneerHacks III is a great opportunity for beginners and advanced programmers to collaborate with friends, win awards, and have fun programming a project you’re passionate about.

Who is the event open to?

PioneerHacks is open to all high school students in grades 9-12 across the globe. If you are a college student/company representative and are interested in mentoring, volunteering, or leading a workshop, make sure to email us at team@pioneerhacks.org to let us know, and we’ll get back to you!

What if I don’t know how to code?

No worries! PioneerHacks III is open to programmers of all experience levels - from total beginners to advanced coders. What matters most isn’t your previous experience, but rather your interest in technology and your passion for creating something cool! There will be plenty of mentors and workshops available to help you out with your project.

What are the team requirements?

You can either create a project individually, or with a team of up to 4 members (including yourself). It’s highly encouraged to join a team, as you’ll be able to have more people working to create a more refined product, and will definitely have a lot of fun with your friends with you!

What should I expect?

You (and your team, if you have one) will be spending an entire weekend designing a product in a remote environment! With the strangeness of COVID-19, PioneerHacks III will (hopefully) be the only hackathon we have to host online-but at least you won't need to bring anything or go anywhere :)

What if I can’t stay?

As PioneerHacks III is remote, we completely understand the wish to not be kept to your device for the duration of the event! Feel free to come and go as you wish--just make sure you catch the opening and closing ceremonies.

Can I code ahead of time?

No. It is against our rules to code before the beginning of the event.
